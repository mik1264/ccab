Fairy Ring Formation

Fairy rings form as fungi grow radially outward from a central point, consuming nutrients and leaving behind self-inhibitory compounds (including self-DNA). This creates the characteristic ring shape rather than a disk. The fungi fruit (produce mushrooms) at the active growth front where mycelium density peaks.
