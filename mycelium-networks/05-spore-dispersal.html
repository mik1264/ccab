Spore Dispersal Dynamics

Fungi release billions of spores into the air. Carried by wind and turbulence, spores follow complex trajectories influenced by gravity, air resistance, and thermal updrafts. Landing on suitable substrate, they germinate into new mycelium colonies. Watch the dispersal kernel emerge -- most spores land nearby, but a few travel remarkably far.
