Gray-Scott Reaction-Diffusion (1-25)

The Gray-Scott model is a mathematical model of an autocatalytic chemical reaction. By varying the feed (F) and kill (k) parameters, it produces an incredible diversity of patterns including spots, stripes, waves, spirals, and chaotic structures—mimicking patterns found throughout nature.

Turing Patterns (26-40)

Alan Turing's groundbreaking 1952 paper "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" proposed that simple chemical systems with activators and inhibitors could spontaneously generate complex patterns. These mechanisms explain animal coat patterns, seashell markings, and vegetation distributions in nature.
