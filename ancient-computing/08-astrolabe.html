Medieval Astrolabe

The astrolabe (Greek: "star-taker") was perfected by Islamic scholars ~800 CE and widely used until the 17th century.

It's a 2D projection of the celestial sphere. The rete (star map) rotates over the tympan (latitude plate) to show star positions at any time.

Click and drag to rotate the rete. Use buttons to adjust time and latitude.
