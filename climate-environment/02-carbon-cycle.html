Global Carbon Cycle

Carbon constantly cycles between atmosphere, oceans, land, and fossil reservoirs. Photosynthesis removes CO₂ from the atmosphere, while respiration, decomposition, and combustion release it back. Human activities have added ~10 GtC/year, disrupting the natural balance and increasing atmospheric CO₂.
