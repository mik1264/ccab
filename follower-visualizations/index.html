About Follower Simulations

These visualizations are based on the classic NetLogo "Follower" model, where autonomous agents (turtles) search for other agents to follow, forming emergent chain structures through simple local rules. Each agent can follow one leader and be followed by one follower, creating dynamic chains that evolve and flow across the canvas.

The core mechanics involve: (1) searching in a donut-shaped radius for unattached agents, (2) forming one-to-one connections when candidates are found, and (3) following the leader's position while unattached agents wander randomly. This simple ruleset creates surprisingly complex and beautiful emergent patterns.
