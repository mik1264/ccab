Nature's Hidden Math

How simple mathematical rules create nature's most beautiful patterns

In 1952, Alan Turing published "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" — a paper that revealed how simple chemical reactions between just two substances could spontaneously create the spots on a leopard, the stripes on a zebra, and the patterns on a seashell.

From the spiral arrangement of sunflower seeds to the branching of coral reefs, from the hexagonal symmetry of snowflakes to the flowing murmurations of starlings — each simulation here demonstrates how breathtaking complexity emerges from astonishingly simple rules. Adjust the parameters and watch nature rebuild itself before your eyes.
