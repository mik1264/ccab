Economic Defendability Theory

For territoriality to evolve, resources must be economically defendable: benefits of exclusive access must outweigh costs of defense. This creates an optimal territory size balancing:

  • Benefits: Food, mates, nesting sites within territory
  • Defense costs: Energy spent patrolling borders, fighting intruders
  • Travel costs: Larger territories require more movement

Key Predictions

  • Higher resource density ‚Üí smaller optimal territories
  • Higher defense costs ‚Üí smaller territories or no territoriality
  • Defense costs scale with perimeter (‚àösize), not area
  • Population pressure creates floaters without territories

Real Examples

This pattern appears across species:

  • Hummingbirds: Smaller territories on flower-rich patches
  • Wolves: Territory size varies with prey density
  • Trout: Adjust territories based on food availability
  • Limpets: Classic demonstrations of optimal territory size
