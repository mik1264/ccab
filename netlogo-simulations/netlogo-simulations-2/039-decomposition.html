Decomposition is the breakdown of dead organic matter, recycling nutrients back to the ecosystem.

Exponential Decay Model

M(t) = M‚ÇÄ √ó e^(-kt)

  • k: Decay constant (varies 0.01-5.0 per year)
  • Half-life = ln(2)/k
  • Controlled by temperature, moisture, litter quality

Two-Phase Decomposition

  • Early phase: Labile compounds (sugars, proteins) decompose fast
  • Late phase: Recalcitrant compounds (lignin) decompose slowly

Controlling Factors

  • Temperature: Higher = faster (up to optimal)
  • Moisture: Intermediate optimal; too dry or wet slows decay
  • C:N ratio: Lower = faster (more N for microbes)
