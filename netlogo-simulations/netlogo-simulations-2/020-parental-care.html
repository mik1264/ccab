This simulation models sexual conflict over parental care ‚Äî one of the fundamental conflicts of interest between the sexes in evolutionary biology.

The Parental Dilemma

Both parents benefit from offspring survival, but providing care is costly only to the caring individual. Each parent does best when the other provides most of the care. This creates a "tug-of-war" between partners.

Game-Theoretic Predictions

Theory predicts biparental care is stable when:

  • Partial compensation: If one parent reduces care, the other increases effort ‚Äî but not enough to fully compensate
  • Neither can succeed alone: One parent alone cannot raise offspring successfully
  • Desertion costs are high: Abandoning offspring significantly reduces fitness

Key Concepts

  • Desertion: Abandoning the brood to seek new mating opportunities
  • Care Level: How much effort each parent invests (0-1)
  • Offspring Need: Total care required for offspring survival
  • Sexual Conflict: Evolutionary interests of males and females differ

Real-World Patterns

In nature, we see remarkable diversity:

  • Birds: Often biparental (90% of species)
  • Fish: Male-only care is common (paternal care)
  • Mammals: Female-only care predominates
  • Penduline Tits: Famous for desertion "games" ‚Äî both parents may abandon!
