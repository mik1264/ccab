‚≠ê Keystone Predation & Trophic Cascades

A keystone species has disproportionately large effects on its ecosystem relative to its abundance. Removing a keystone predator triggers a trophic cascade.

The Classic Experiment

Robert Paine's experiments on Washington coast showed that when the starfish Pisaster was removed, mussels outcompeted all other species, reducing diversity from 15+ species to near-monoculture.

Trophic Cascade Mechanism

  • With predator: Dominant competitor is controlled ‚Üí diverse community
  • Without predator: Dominant competitor monopolizes resources ‚Üí competitive exclusion
  • Cascade effect: Changes ripple through multiple trophic levels

Other Examples

  • Sea otters: Control urchins, protecting kelp forests
  • Wolves: Control elk, allowing vegetation recovery
  • Sharks: Control mesopredators, protecting seagrass
