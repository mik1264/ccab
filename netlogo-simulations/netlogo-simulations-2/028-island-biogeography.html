MacArthur and Wilson's Theory of Island Biogeography (1967) explains species diversity on islands through the balance of immigration and extinction.

Key Predictions

  • Distance effect: Farther islands have lower immigration rates
  • Area effect: Smaller islands have higher extinction rates
  • Equilibrium: Species number stabilizes where immigration = extinction
  • Turnover: Species composition changes even at equilibrium
