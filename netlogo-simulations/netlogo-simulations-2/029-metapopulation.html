A metapopulation is a "population of populations" ‚Äî local populations in habitat patches connected by dispersal.

Levins Model

dp/dt = cp(1-p) - ep

At equilibrium: p* = 1 - e/c

Key Concepts

  • Colonization: Empty patches colonized by dispersers
  • Local extinction: Populations in patches go extinct
  • Rescue effect: Immigration prevents extinction
  • Source-sink: Some patches export, others import individuals

Conservation Implications

Habitat fragmentation creates metapopulation dynamics. Maintaining connectivity is crucial for species persistence.
