A lek is an aggregation of males gathered to perform competitive courtship displays. Females visit leks to compare males and choose mates. This results in extreme mating skew ‚Äî a few males get nearly all matings.

Why Do Males Aggregate?

Three main hypotheses explain lek formation:

  • Hotspot Hypothesis: Males gather where females naturally pass through
  • Hotshot Hypothesis: Subordinate males cluster around attractive "hotshot" males to intercept females
  • Female Preference: Females prefer to mate in groups (easier comparison, reduced predation)

Extreme Mating Skew

In real leks, the top 5-10% of males often achieve 70-90% of all matings! This intense female choice drives evolution of elaborate male ornaments:

  • Peacocks: Elaborate tail displays
  • Sage Grouse: Inflatable chest sacs
  • Birds of Paradise: Colorful plumage and dances
  • Cichlid Fish: Sand bower construction

Model Dynamics

  • Males display with varying quality (genetic + condition)
  • Females sample multiple males before choosing
  • Subordinates cluster near hotshots
  • Female choosiness affects mating skew
