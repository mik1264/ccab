Coral reefs are among the most biodiverse ecosystems on Earth, but face threats from warming, acidification, and overfishing.

Coral-Algae Competition

  • Corals and macroalgae compete for space
  • Herbivorous fish control algae growth
  • Nutrient enrichment favors algae
  • Reduced herbivory ‚Üí algal dominance

Bleaching

  • Heat stress expels symbiotic zooxanthellae
  • Coral loses color and energy source
  • Can recover if stress is brief
  • Prolonged stress ‚Üí coral death

Phase Shifts

Reefs can shift from coral-dominated to algae-dominated states. These shifts may be difficult to reverse (hysteresis).
