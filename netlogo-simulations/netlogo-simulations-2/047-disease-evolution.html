Pathogens evolve under selection pressure, often toward an optimal virulence level determined by transmission-virulence tradeoffs.

Virulence-Transmission Tradeoff

  • Higher virulence ‚Üí more pathogen replication ‚Üí higher transmission
  • But also ‚Üí faster host death ‚Üí less time to transmit
  • Intermediate virulence often optimal

Evolutionary Predictions

  • Vector-borne diseases: Can evolve high virulence (don't need mobile hosts)
  • Directly transmitted: Often evolve toward intermediate virulence
  • Competition within host: Selects for higher virulence

Immune Escape

Pathogens also evolve to escape host immunity, driving antigenic drift (flu) and variant emergence (COVID-19).
