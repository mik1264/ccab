Ecosystem engineers are organisms that create, significantly modify, maintain, or destroy habitats, affecting many other species.

Types of Engineers

  • Allogenic: Modify environment by transforming materials (beavers building dams)
  • Autogenic: Change environment via their own physical presence (coral reefs, trees)

Beaver Example

  • Dams retain water, creating ponds and wetlands
  • Increase habitat heterogeneity
  • Biodiversity often 10-100x higher in engineered habitats
  • Legacy effects persist decades after engineers leave

Cascade Effects

Engineering affects hydrology, nutrient cycling, sediment dynamics, and species composition far beyond the immediate modification site.
