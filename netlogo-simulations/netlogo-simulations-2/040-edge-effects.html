Edge effects are ecological changes that occur at the boundary between two habitats, extending into the habitat interior.

Types of Effects

  • Abiotic: Changed microclimate (light, wind, temperature, humidity)
  • Direct biological: Species changes due to physical conditions
  • Indirect biological: Changed species interactions (predation, parasitism)

Edge Penetration

  • Effects can extend 100-300m into forest fragments
  • Small fragments may have no "core" habitat
  • Shape matters: elongated fragments have more edge

Matrix Effects

The surrounding landscape (matrix) affects edge intensity. Hostile matrix (agriculture, urban) creates stronger edge effects than benign matrix (secondary forest).
