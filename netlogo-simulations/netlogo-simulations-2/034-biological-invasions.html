Biological invasion occurs when non-native species establish, spread, and cause ecological or economic harm in a new environment.

Invasion Stages

  • Introduction: Species arrives (often human-mediated)
  • Establishment: Self-sustaining population forms
  • Lag Phase: Slow growth while adapting
  • Spread: Rapid range expansion

Key Factors

  • Propagule pressure: More introductions ‚Üí higher success
  • Enemy release: Escape from natural predators/pathogens
  • Disturbance: Creates openings for invasion
  • Biotic resistance: Native community can resist invaders

Spread Dynamics

Invasions typically show exponential range expansion after lag phase, with diffusion-like spread from introduction points.
