Gause's Principle: Two species competing for identical resources cannot stably coexist ‚Äî one will drive the other extinct.

The Solution: Niche Partitioning

Species can coexist by specializing on different resources or using shared resources differently:

  • Resource partitioning: Different food types or sizes
  • Spatial partitioning: Different habitat locations
  • Temporal partitioning: Active at different times

Real Examples

  • Warblers: Feed in different parts of the same tree
  • Anole lizards: Occupy different heights in forests
  • Darwin's finches: Different beak sizes for different seeds

The Paradox of the Plankton

Many plankton species coexist on few resources, violating Gause's principle ‚Äî likely due to environmental fluctuations preventing equilibrium.
