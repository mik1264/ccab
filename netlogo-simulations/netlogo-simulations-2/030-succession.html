Ecological succession is the gradual process of ecosystem change following disturbance, from bare ground to climax community.

Stages

  • Pioneer: Lichens, mosses colonize bare rock/soil
  • Early: Grasses, herbaceous plants
  • Mid: Shrubs, shade-intolerant trees
  • Climax: Shade-tolerant trees, stable community

Mechanisms

  • Facilitation: Early species improve conditions for later species
  • Tolerance: Later species can grow despite early species
  • Inhibition: Early species prevent establishment of later species

Examples

After volcanic eruption (primary succession) or fire (secondary succession), communities gradually recover toward a climax state.
