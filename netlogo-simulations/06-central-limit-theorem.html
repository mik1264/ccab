üìä Central Limit Theorem

The Central Limit Theorem is one of the most important results in statistics. It states that when you take sample means from any population, they form a normal (bell-shaped) distribution‚Äîregardless of the original population's shape.

Why It Matters

  • Foundation of statistics: Enables reliable predictions without knowing the true distribution
  • Universal applicability: Works for virtually any underlying distribution
  • Sample size rule: n ‚â• 30 is typically sufficient for the theorem to apply

The Mathematics

If you repeatedly sample and calculate means:

  • Sample means center around the true population mean
  • ~68% fall within 1 standard deviation of the mean
  • ~95% fall within 2 standard deviations
  • The spread narrows as sample size increases

Real-World Applications

  • Election polling: Predicting outcomes from voter surveys
  • Quality control: Estimating defect rates from samples
  • Medical trials: Testing drug efficacy across patients

What to Observe

  • Start with any distribution shape (uniform, skewed, etc.)
  • Watch the histogram of sample means become bell-shaped
  • Notice the distribution narrows with larger samples
