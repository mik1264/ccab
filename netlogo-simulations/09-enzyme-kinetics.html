üß¨ Enzyme Kinetics

Enzyme kinetics describes how fast enzyme-catalyzed reactions proceed. The Michaelis-Menten model explains the relationship between substrate concentration and reaction velocity.

The Basic Reaction

E + S ‚áå ES ‚Üí E + P
(Enzyme + Substrate ‚áå Complex ‚Üí Enzyme + Product)

Key Parameters

  • Km (Michaelis Constant): Substrate concentration at half-maximum velocity. Lower Km = higher enzyme affinity
  • Vmax: Maximum reaction rate when enzyme is fully saturated
  • Substrate [S]: The molecule the enzyme acts upon

The Equation

v = (Vmax √ó [S]) / (Km + [S])

Applications

  • Drug design: Inhibitors block enzymes to treat disease
  • Diagnostics: Enzyme activity indicates metabolic state
  • Industry: Optimize enzymatic manufacturing

What to Observe

  • Rate increases linearly at low substrate, then plateaus
  • Saturation occurs when all enzyme is busy
  • More enzyme = higher Vmax
