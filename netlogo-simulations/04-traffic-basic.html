This simulation implements the Nagel-Schreckenberg model, a cellular automaton that explains how traffic jams can appear "out of nowhere" without any obvious cause.

The Model Rules

Each car follows simple rules each timestep:

  • Accelerate: Speed up if there's room ahead
  • Slow down: Brake if too close to the car ahead
  • Random slowdown: Occasionally slow for no reason (models human behavior)

Phantom Traffic Jams

The key insight is that small perturbations amplify backward:

  • One car brakes slightly harder than necessary
  • The car behind brakes even harder (overreaction)
  • This chain reaction propagates backward through traffic
  • Eventually, cars at the back come to a complete stop

Phase Transitions

The system exhibits distinct behaviors based on density:

  • Low density: Free flow, all cars at max speed
  • Medium density: Unstable, jams form and dissolve
  • High density: Stop-and-go waves, persistent jams
