Radioactive decay is the spontaneous breakdown of unstable atomic nuclei. It's a fundamentally stochastic (random) process‚Äîwe can't predict when any single atom will decay, only the probability.

Key Concepts

  • Half-life: Time for half the atoms to decay

  • Decay constant (Œª): Probability of decay per unit time

Why It's Random

Each atom has an equal probability of decaying in any time interval. This leads to:

  • Predictable average behavior (law of large numbers)
  • Unpredictable individual events
  • Poisson-distributed decay counts

Applications

  • Carbon dating: Measuring age of organic material
  • Nuclear medicine: Diagnostic imaging and treatment
  • Power generation: Nuclear reactors

What to Observe

  • Exponential curve emerges from random individual decays
  • Small samples show noisy behavior; large samples are smooth
  • Half-life is constant regardless of starting amount
