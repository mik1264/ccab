Atanasoff-Berry Computer

1942 — Iowa State College

Built by John Atanasoff and Clifford Berry, the ABC was the first automatic electronic digital computer. It used rotating capacitor drums to store 60 numbers of 50 bits each, and vacuum tube logic circuits for binary arithmetic.

Though it could only solve systems of linear equations, it pioneered electronic digital computing, binary arithmetic, and regenerative capacitor memory.
