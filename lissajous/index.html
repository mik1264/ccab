Lissajous curves are the elegant patterns traced by the intersection of two perpendicular harmonic oscillations. Named after Jules Antoine Lissajous, these mathematical curves reveal the hidden beauty of frequency relationships and phase differences.

When two sine waves with different frequencies and phases combine, they create mesmerizing figures that have captivated mathematicians, physicists, and artists for over a century.
