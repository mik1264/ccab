About the X5.1 Solar Flare

On November 11, 2025, the Sun erupted with an X5.1-class solar flare from sunspot AR4274, marking the most intense solar flare of the year. This powerful event caused radio blackouts over Africa and Europe and was accompanied by a fast-moving coronal mass ejection (CME) expected to impact Earth within 15-18 hours.

This gallery presents 40 interactive visualizations exploring every aspect of this significant space weather event, from the flare's physical mechanisms to its impacts on Earth and human technology.
