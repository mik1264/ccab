What Are Agent-Based Macroeconomic Models?

Agent-Based Models (ABMs) of the macroeconomy represent economies as complex systems where macro variables like GDP, unemployment, and inflation emerge from the decentralized decisions of heterogeneous agents‚Äîhouseholds, firms, and banks. Unlike traditional equilibrium models (DSGE/RBC), ABMs can model:

  • Out-of-equilibrium dynamics and path-dependent outcomes
  • Heterogeneous agents with different wealth, income, and behavioral rules
  • Credit frictions and balance sheet constraints
  • Network effects and spatial interactions
  • Emergent crises from cascading defaults and bank failures

These models follow the ODD (Overview-Design-Details) Protocol for reproducibility and are used by central banks, policy institutions, and researchers to test monetary/fiscal interventions and understand stylized facts like the Phillips Curve, Okun's Law, and wealth inequality dynamics.
