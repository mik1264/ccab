College Admissions (Many-to-One)

The Gale-Shapley algorithm extends naturally to college admissions where each college has a quota of seats. Students propose to colleges, and colleges hold the best applicants up to their quota, rejecting the rest.

Algorithm Pseudocode 
College Admissions (Student-Proposing):

1. Each unmatched student proposes to
   his highest-ranked college he has
   not yet applied to.

2. Each college considers all current
   holders + new applicants. It holds
   the top q (quota) applicants and
   rejects the rest.

3. Rejected students become free and
   apply to their next choice.

4. Repeat until no free students remain.

This produces the student-optimal
stable assignment.
"Consider a set of colleges and applicants where each college has a quota. We seek an assignment of applicants to colleges which is stable." — Gale & Shapley, 1962, Section 4
