Moire Patterns

Visual Interference and Emergent Beauty

When two repetitive patterns overlap with slightly different frequencies or angles, something magical happens: large-scale interference fringes emerge from the interaction of fine-scale structures. These moire patterns appear everywhere — in screen doors, fabric weaves, printed halftones, and precision metrology. They are the visual analog of musical beats, turning tiny differences into sweeping visual waves. Move your mouse, twist the sliders, and watch interference paint itself across the screen.
