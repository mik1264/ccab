Music Acoustics

Waves, harmonics, resonance, and sound synthesis

About Music Acoustics Simulations

Explore the physics of sound and music through interactive visualizations. From wave interference patterns and harmonic series to resonance phenomena and digital sound synthesis, these simulations reveal the science behind what we hear. Play with frequencies, watch standing waves form, and create sounds using various synthesis techniques.

01

Wave Interference

Superposition of two waves. Observe constructive/destructive interference and beat frequencies.

Waves 02

Harmonics & Timbre

Fourier synthesis with adjustable harmonic amplitudes. Build sawtooth, square, and custom waveforms.

Synthesis 03

Resonance

Driven harmonic oscillator with damping. Watch the frequency response peak at natural frequency.

Vibration 04

String Vibration

Standing wave modes on a fixed string. Visualize nodes, antinodes, and harmonic frequencies.

Vibration 05

Doppler Effect

Frequency shift from relative motion. Watch wave compression and expansion as source moves.

Waves 06

Spectrum Analyzer

Real-time FFT visualization. See frequency content of synthesized sounds in bars, lines, or spectrogram.

Analysis 07

Room Acoustics

Ray tracing for sound reflections. Visualize echoes, reverb time (RT60), and impulse response.

Acoustics 08

Sound Synthesis

Subtractive, FM, and additive synthesis with ADSR envelopes. Play a virtual keyboard.
