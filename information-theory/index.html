Information Theory

Shannon entropy, coding, compression, and channel capacity

About Information Theory Simulations

Explore the mathematical foundations of communication and data processing. From Shannon's groundbreaking entropy formula to Huffman coding and error correction, these simulations visualize the abstract concepts that underpin all digital communication. Understand how information is measured, compressed, transmitted through noisy channels, and recovered with remarkable reliability.

Error Correction 04

Channel Capacity

Shannon-Hartley theorem: C = B log₂(1 + S/N). Visualize how bandwidth and SNR determine capacity.

Compression 06

Mutual Information

I(X;Y) = H(X) + H(Y) - H(X,Y). Visualize shared information between correlated random variables.

Explore lossless compression limits. The typical set and asymptotic equipartition property explained.

Theory 08

Binary Symmetric Channel

The canonical noisy channel model. Transmit bits through a BSC and observe the capacity C = 1 - H(p).
