The SEIR model adds an Exposed (latent) compartment between Susceptible and Infectious.

Disease States

  • S: Susceptible - can become infected
  • E: Exposed - infected but not yet infectious
  • I: Infectious - can transmit disease
  • R: Recovered - immune

Key Differences from SIR

  • Delayed epidemic peak
  • Lower, broader infection curve
  • More realistic for diseases with incubation periods
  • Examples: COVID-19, measles, chickenpox

Parameters

œÉ (sigma) = 1/incubation period. Higher œÉ means shorter latent period, approaching SIR dynamics.
