Physics: Geometric optics traces light as rays that travel in straight lines and bend according to Snell's law at interfaces. Convex lenses focus parallel rays to a point (real focus), while concave lenses diverge them (virtual focus). Prisms disperse white light into its spectrum due to wavelength-dependent refraction.
