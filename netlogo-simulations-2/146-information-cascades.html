Information Cascades

Information cascades occur when individuals make decisions sequentially by observing others' choices rather than their own private information.

Key dynamics:

  • Bayesian updating: Each agent combines private signal with public history
  • Cascade formation: When public info overwhelms private signals
  • Fragility: Cascades can form on wrong choices and collapse suddenly
  • Herding: Rational behavior can lead to suboptimal outcomes
