How Carrier Recovery Works

Problem: Coherent demodulation requires local carrier synchronized to transmitted carrier in both frequency and phase.

Phase Detector: Multiplies input with VCO output. DC component proportional to phase difference.

Loop Filter: Averages phase error, removes high-frequency components.

VCO: Voltage-Controlled Oscillator adjusts frequency based on filtered error.

Lock: When phase error stays small, PLL is "locked" - carrier is recovered!

Costas Loop: Special PLL that works with suppressed-carrier signals (BPSK, QPSK).
