About Boids Algorithm

The Boids algorithm, developed by Craig Reynolds in 1986, simulates the flocking behavior of birds through three simple rules applied locally by each agent. Despite having no central coordination or leader, these rules produce remarkably lifelike emergent collective motion.

  • Separation: Steer away from nearby flockmates to avoid collisions (priority rule)
  • Alignment: Match the average heading of visible neighbors using trigonometric averaging
  • Cohesion: Move toward the average position of the local flock

Each visualization demonstrates different aspects of flocking behavior, from basic implementations to advanced features like predator avoidance, 3D motion, and interactive parameter exploration.
