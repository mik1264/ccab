⚜️ Renaissance Economics Simulation ⚜️

Agent-Based Modeling of European Renaissance Economy (1400-1600 CE)

Explore the economic dynamics of the Renaissance period through interactive agent-based simulations. These models demonstrate trade networks, banking innovations, wealth distribution, and the complex economic systems that fueled Europe's cultural and artistic golden age.

Key Features of Renaissance EconSim

  • Agent-based modeling with merchants, bankers, artisans, and nobles
  • Historical accuracy based on Renaissance economic records
  • Interactive parameters to explore "what-if" scenarios
  • Real-time visualization of economic activity
  • Banking innovations: double-entry bookkeeping, bills of exchange
  • Trade network dynamics between major city-states
  • Wealth distribution and inequality metrics (Gini coefficient)
  • Historical events: plagues, wars, jubilees
  • Educational insights into economic history
  • No build required - pure HTML/JavaScript simulations

5 Renaissance Economic Simulations

Each simulation focuses on a different aspect of Renaissance economics, from basic agent-based trade to complex banking systems and city-state economies. Inspired by NetLogo-style modeling, these visualizations bring historical economic concepts to life.

Version 1
Basic Agent-Based Trade
Grid-based simulation with merchants, bankers, and artisans moving and trading. Watch economic agents interact, trade goods, and accumulate wealth in real-time.
Version 2
Trade Network Visualization
Network visualization of 12 major Renaissance city-states (Florence, Venice, Genoa, etc.). Trade routes, goods flow, tariffs, and historical events like plagues.
Version 3
Wealth Distribution Dynamics
Comprehensive inequality simulation with Gini coefficient, Lorenz curves, histograms, and time-series analysis. Explore tax policies and inheritance effects.
Version 4
Banking & Credit System
Medici-style banking simulation with loans, interest rates, defaults, and fractional reserve banking. Watch the credit multiplier in action.
Version 5
Florence City-State Economy
Complete simulation of Florence's economy with 6 sectors: textiles, banking, arts, crafts, agriculture, and trade. Historical events and patron system.

Historical Context

The Renaissance (14th-17th centuries) was not just an artistic revolution but an economic one. Italian city-states like Florence, Venice, and Genoa pioneered modern banking, international trade, and financial instruments. The Medici family's innovations in double-entry bookkeeping and bills of exchange laid the groundwork for modern capitalism. These simulations explore the economic mechanisms that funded masterpieces by Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and countless others, while also revealing the stark inequalities of the era.
