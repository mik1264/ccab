When 23 strangers walk into a room...

The Surprise: In a room of just 23 people, there's a 50% chance that two share the same birthday!

ü§î Test Your Intuition First

How many people do you think you'd need for a 50% chance of a shared birthday?

The Counterintuitive Truth

Most people guess around 183 (half of 365). After all, if there are 365 possible birthdays, shouldn't you need about half that many people?

The key insight: you're not comparing one person to everyone else. You're comparing every pair of people. With 23 people, there are 253 pairs to check!

üß† Why Our Intuition Fails

We instinctively think about one specific person matching another specific birthday. The chance of that is indeed tiny: 1/365 ‚âà 0.27%.

But we're asking a different question: "Does any pair match?" With n people, there are n(n-1)/2 pairs. At n=23, that's 253 opportunities for a match!
