üîÑ The Reversal

A trend in every subgroup can reverse when groups are combined! This happens when a confounding variable (department choice) correlates with both the grouping variable (gender) and the outcome (admission).

üéì Berkeley 1973

Overall: Men 44%, Women 35% admitted. Looks like bias! But women applied more to competitive departments (humanities, social sciences) while men applied to easier ones (STEM with high acceptance).

‚ö†Ô∏è Why It Matters

Simpson's paradox leads to wrong policy decisions. Medical trials, educational studies, and legal cases have been affected. Always ask: "What happens when we break down by subgroups?"
