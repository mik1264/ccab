üé≤ The Paradox

Die A beats B. Die B beats C. Die C beats D. So A beats D? NO! Die D beats A! This violates our intuition about "better than" being transitive. It's like Rock-Paper-Scissors with dice!

This paradox shows that the relation "wins more than half the time" is not transitive. Martin Gardner featured Efron's dice in Scientific American (1970), sparking widespread interest in nontransitive games.
