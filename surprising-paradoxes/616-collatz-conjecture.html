Repeat until reaching 1. Does every number reach 1? UNSOLVED since 1937!

Hailstone Numbers

Like hailstones in a cloud, numbers bounce up and down unpredictably before finally falling to 1. The number 27 climbs to 9232 before eventually descending—a 342× increase from a tiny starting point!

Deceptively Simple

Paul Erdős: "Mathematics may not be ready for such problems." Jeffrey Lagarias: "completely out of reach of present day mathematics." The rule takes ONE LINE to write but has resisted proof for 87+ years.

Verified to 2.36×10²¹

Every number tested reaches 1. In 2020, Terence Tao proved "almost all" numbers satisfy the conjecture. But "almost all" isn't "all"—a single counterexample would disprove the conjecture forever!

Record Holders

Numbers that take longest to reach 1: 27 (111 steps), 97 (118), 871 (178), 6171 (261), 77031 (350), 837799 (524 steps)! Small numbers can have surprisingly long journeys.
