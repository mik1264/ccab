The Streisand Effect

When Censorship Makes Things Go Viral

The Paradox

Attempting to hide, remove, or censor information often causes it to spread far more widely than if left alone. The very act of suppression draws attention to what's being suppressed.

Named after singer Barbra Streisand, who in 2003 sued a photographer for $50 million to remove an aerial photo of her Malibu mansion from a coastal erosion database. The result?
