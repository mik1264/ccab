Survivorship Bias

The Logical Error of Ignoring the Dead

We only see the survivors, never the failures. This leads us to wildly wrong conclusions.

The most famous example? Abraham Wald and the WWII bombers.

Abraham Wald (1902-1950)

Born in Austria-Hungary, Wald was a brilliant mathematician who fled Nazi persecution in 1938 after the Anschluss. He emigrated to the US, where he joined Columbia University and later the Statistical Research Group (SRG) during WWII.

His work on sequential analysis and survivorship bias in bomber armor became foundational to operations research‚Äîa field that applies mathematical methods to decision-making.
