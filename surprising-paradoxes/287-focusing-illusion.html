Kahneman found that simply changing question ORDER dramatically changes the correlation between dating life and overall happiness:

What happened? When dating is asked first, it becomes salient‚Äî you're now thinking about it when you rate your life. The dating question primes you to weight dating heavily. Same people, same lives, wildly different correlation!

The Income Illusion

"Would you be happier if you were richer?" Most say yes. Reality says... barely.

Above ~$75K/year (2010 dollars), income has almost zero effect on day-to-day emotional well-being. The rich report higher "life satisfaction" (a cognitive judgment) but aren't actually happier in moment-to-moment experience. They're more tense and spend no more time in enjoyable activities.

Why We Fall for the Focusing Illusion

When you think about any factor‚Äîincome, location, health, relationships‚Äîyou exaggerate its importance simply because you're thinking about it. In reality, that factor competes with thousands of others for influence on your mood.

This explains why lottery winners return to baseline happiness, why paraplegics are happier than expected, and why that promotion won't transform your life. You adapt. Other things matter. But while focused on the change, you can't see this.
