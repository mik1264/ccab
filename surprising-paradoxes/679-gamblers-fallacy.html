Why We Fall For It

Pattern Recognition Gone Wrong: Our brains evolved to find patterns ‚Äî it helped our ancestors survive. But this instinct misfires with truly random events.

The "Law of Averages" Myth: People believe outcomes must "balance out" in the short term. They do ‚Äî but only over VERY long periods, and not by compensating for past results.

The Hot Hand's Evil Twin: The Gambler's Fallacy expects reversals after streaks. The Hot Hand Fallacy expects streaks to continue. Both are wrong for independent events!
