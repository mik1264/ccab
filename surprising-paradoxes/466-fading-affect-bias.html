The Science Behind the Bias

The Fading Affect Bias (FAB) is a robust phenomenon documented across cultures and age groups. Negative emotions associated with autobiographical memories tend to fade faster than positive emotions.

Why does this happen? Several mechanisms contribute:

‚Ä¢ Emotional regulation: We naturally work to minimize negative feelings, rehearsing and reframing bad memories in ways that reduce their sting.

‚Ä¢ Social sharing: Talking about negative events helps process and diminish their emotional impact, while sharing positive memories often enhances them.

‚Ä¢ Evolutionary advantage: Maintaining positive affect supports approach behaviors, social connection, and resilience ‚Äî key survival traits.

‚Ä¢ Self-enhancement: We're motivated to view ourselves positively, which includes remembering our past in a favorable light.
