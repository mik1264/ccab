The Paradox

In 1968, psychologist Robert Zajonc demonstrated something counterintuitive: we don't like things because they're good‚Äîwe like them because they're familiar.

He showed participants meaningless Chinese characters they'd never seen before. Some characters appeared once, others appeared up to 25 times. When asked to rate which characters had "more positive meanings," participants consistently chose the ones they'd seen more often‚Äîeven though they had no idea what they meant.

The Dark Side

The effect has troubling implications: We don't just prefer familiar things, we trust them. Misinformation repeated often enough feels true (the "illusory truth effect"). Propaganda works not by convincing, but by familiarizing.
