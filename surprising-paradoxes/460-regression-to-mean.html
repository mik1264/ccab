But the truth: exceptional performances (good OR bad) are followed by regression toward the mean, regardless of feedback.

The Saddening Truth (Kahneman)

"We normally praise good behavior and punish bad. By regression alone, performance is most likely to improve after punishment and deteriorate after reward. We are exposed to a lifetime schedule in which we are most often rewarded for punishing and punished for rewarding."

Why Does This Happen?

Statistical Inevitability: Extreme scores have luck as a component. A 90 score likely had good luck; regression means the next score will probably be lower even without intervention.

The Illusion: After an excellent performance, ANY feedback (or none) will be followed by a decline. After terrible performance, ANY feedback will be followed by improvement. We attribute this to our actions, but it's just math.

Real-World Consequences: Sports "slumps" after stellar seasons. The "sophomore jinx." Why therapy seems to work (people seek help at extreme low points). Why get-tough policies seem effective (crime was already at a peak).
