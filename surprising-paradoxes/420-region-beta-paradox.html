Worse Situations Can Heal FASTER

Intense distress triggers psychological defense mechanisms. Mild distress doesn't. Result: you recover from terrible experiences faster than merely bad ones.

Like the commuter who only cycles past 1 mile, your psychological immune system only activates for sufficiently intense threats.

üõ°Ô∏è Defense Mechanisms

  • Cognitive Reframing "This is a growth opportunity" ‚Äî only triggered by major setbacks
  • Social Support Mobilization Friends rally around crises, not minor complaints
  • Problem-Solving Mode "This must change" ‚Äî requires sufficient urgency
  • Rationalization "They don't matter anyway" ‚Äî needs strong threat to activate

üìö Real-World Examples

  • Relationships: "Not great but not bad enough to leave" relationships last for years
  • Careers: Mediocre jobs persist; toxic ones get quit quickly
  • Terrorism: Major attacks trigger resilience; minor threats cause lingering anxiety
  • Health: Serious diagnoses mobilize action; mild symptoms get ignored
