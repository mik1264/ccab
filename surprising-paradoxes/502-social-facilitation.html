üìú Historical Timeline

1898

Norman Triplett

First scientific study of social facilitation. Observed cyclists rode faster when racing others than when alone. Replicated with children winding fishing reels‚Äîfaster in pairs!

1965

Robert Zajonc's Drive Theory

Proposed that arousal from others' presence increases the "dominant response"‚Äîwhatever you'd naturally do first. For well-learned tasks, that's correct; for new tasks, it's wrong.

1969

Cockroach Experiment

Zajonc, Heingartner & Herman: Cockroaches ran straight runways FASTER with an audience but completed mazes SLOWER. Proved the effect applies even to simple organisms without self-awareness.

1983

Meta-Analysis Confirmation

Bond & Titus analyzed 200+ studies with 20,000+ participants. Confirmed: audiences significantly improve simple task performance and significantly hurt complex task performance.
