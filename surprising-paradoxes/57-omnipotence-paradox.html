The Omnipotence Paradox

"The stone too heavy for God to lift"

This ancient paradox challenges the very concept of unlimited power. If a being is truly all-powerful, can it limit its own power? The question has puzzled theologians and philosophers for over a thousand years.

"Can God create a stone so heavy that even He cannot lift it?"

1. Aquinas's Logical Limits

Omnipotence means the power to do anything logically possible. Creating a "stone too heavy for an omnipotent being to lift" is self-contradictory‚Äîlike a "square circle." God cannot do the logically impossible, not because of weakness, but because such things are meaningless.

2. Mavrodes's Defense

If God is omnipotent, then "a stone too heavy for God to lift" is a contradiction. Asking God to create one is like asking Him to make 2+2=5. The question isn't a real limit on power‚Äîit's a linguistic trick.

‚Äî George Mavrodes, 1963

3. Descartes's Absolute Power

God CAN do the logically impossible! He could create the stone AND lift it simultaneously. Logic itself is created by God and doesn't bind Him. The paradox only shows our limited understanding.

4. Mackie's Orders of Omnipotence

Distinguish first-order omnipotence (power to act) from second-order omnipotence (power to modify powers). God could use second-order power to limit His first-order power‚Äîthen genuinely be unable to lift the stone.

‚Äî J.L. Mackie, 1955

üîÑ Why It's Paradoxical

The paradox creates a dilemma with no escape:

  • If YES: God creates the stone. Now there's something He cannot do (lift it). Therefore He's not omnipotent.
  • If NO: God cannot create such a stone. There's something He cannot do (create it). Therefore He's not omnipotent.

Either answer seems to disprove omnipotence. The very concept appears to be self-contradictory!

üîÄ Related Paradoxes

The omnipotence paradox has many variations:

  • The Irresistible Force Paradox: What happens when an irresistible force meets an immovable object?
  • Self-Binding: Can an omnipotent being make a promise it cannot break?
  • Knowledge Limits: Can God create a secret He doesn't know?
  • Evil and Free Will: Can God create beings with genuine free will He cannot override?

Each variation probes the same fundamental tension: can unlimited power limit itself?
