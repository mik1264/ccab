The Puzzle

The Setup

  • An island with N people, K have blue eyes
  • Everyone can see everyone else's eyes
  • No one knows their own eye color (no mirrors, no discussion)
  • Rule: If you know your eyes are blue, you must leave at midnight
  • All islanders are perfect logicians

The Event

A visitor says: "I see at least one person with blue eyes."

But everyone already knew this! (They can all see the blue-eyed people.)

What happens?

The Answer

On day K, all K blue-eyed islanders leave simultaneously!

The visitor's "trivial" statement kills them all.

Day-by-Day Logic

Day 0
Visitor announces: "I see blue eyes"

The Inductive Proof

Base Case: K = 1

If there's only ONE blue-eyed person, they see NO blue eyes. After the announcement ("at least one"), they deduce: "It must be me!" ‚Üí They leave on Day 1.

Inductive Step: K = 2

Each blue-eyed person sees exactly ONE other blue-eyed person. They think: "If that's the only one, they'll leave on Day 1."

Day 1 passes. No one leaves. Each blue-eyed person deduces: "There must be TWO blue-eyed people. I must be the other one!" ‚Üí Both leave on Day 2.

General Case: K = N

Each blue-eyed person sees N-1 other blue-eyed people. They think: "If there are only N-1, they'll all leave on Day N-1."

Day N-1 passes. No one leaves. Each deduces: "There must be N blue-eyed people. I'm one of them!" ‚Üí All N leave on Day N.

The Deep Truth: Common Knowledge

Mutual Knowledge vs Common Knowledge

Before the announcement: Everyone KNOWS there are blue-eyed people. (Mutual knowledge)

After the announcement: Everyone knows that everyone knows that everyone knows... (Common knowledge)

The visitor's statement seems redundant, but it creates an infinite tower of knowledge that wasn't there before.

Why the Induction Requires Common Knowledge

For K=3, person A must reason: "Person B is reasoning about what Person C is reasoning about what Person B is reasoning about..."

This recursive reasoning requires everyone to know that everyone knows the base fact. The announcement creates this anchor.

The Paradox

The visitor stated something everyone already knew. Yet it changed everything. This is because "everyone knows X" ‚â† "everyone knows everyone knows X".

The announcement synchronized their knowledge, allowing the inductive chain to complete.
