üß† The Neuroscience of Bistable Perception

The Necker Cube reveals something profound: your brain constructs reality, it doesn't just passively receive it. The retinal image never changes, yet your perception flips between two mutually exclusive interpretations.

This happens because the 2D drawing contains no depth cues. Your visual cortex must "guess" which interpretation is correct‚Äîand when neither wins decisively, it alternates between them every few seconds.

Fascinatingly, the flip is hard to control voluntarily. You can try to "hold" one interpretation, but eventually your perception will flip despite your intention‚Äî revealing limits on conscious control over perception.
