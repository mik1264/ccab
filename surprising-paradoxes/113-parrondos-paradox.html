ü§Ø The Mind-Bending Paradox

In 1996, Spanish physicist Juan Parrondo discovered something that seems impossible: two games that each lose money on average can be combined into a winning strategy!

üé≤ The Paradox

Game A loses money. Game B loses money.
But alternating between A and B... WINS money!
LOSING + LOSING = WINNING?!

üé≤ Game A (Simple Coin Flip)

Win $1 49.5%
Lose $1 50.5%
Expected value -$0.01/game

üé≤ Game B (Capital-Dependent)

If capital divisible by 3:

Win $1 9.5% (bad coin!)

Otherwise:

Win $1 74.5% (good coin!)
Expected value -$0.017/game

The secret is that Game A's random fluctuations keep your capital away from multiples of 3, so when you play Game B, you're more likely to use the good coin (74.5% win) instead of the bad coin (9.5% win). It's like a ratchet that only lets you move upward!
