The Paradox

If buses arrive every 10 minutes on average, your average wait isn't 5 minutes...
It's longer! You're more likely to arrive during a long gap between buses.

Historical Note

The inspection paradox was formalized by William Feller in his classic 1971 textbook on probability theory. It's a form of size-biased sampling ‚Äî whenever you sample something proportional to its size, larger items are overrepresented. Real data from Boston's MBTA Red Line showed passengers experience 18% longer wait times than the schedule suggests due to this effect.
