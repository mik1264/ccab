üß† Why Ownership Changes Value

Loss aversion: Losing something hurts ~2x more than gaining the same thing feels good. When you own it, selling becomes a LOSS.

Psychological ownership: Just thinking of something as "mine" triggers the effect‚Äîeven for things we never physically possessed (ideas, ticket claims).

Status quo bias: We prefer our current state. Selling changes the status quo; keeping things doesn't. The default wins.

The Science

Richard Thaler coined the term "endowment effect" in 1980. He later won the Nobel Prize in Economics (2017), partly for this work on behavioral economics.

The paradox: Standard economic theory assumes consistent preferences‚Äîyou'd value something the same whether buying or selling. The endowment effect proves this wrong: ownership fundamentally changes valuation.

Market implications: This is why there's a gap between bid and ask prices. Why people hold losing stocks too long. Why negotiations stall. We're not trading objects‚Äîwe're trading parts of our identity.

Possession isn't 9/10 of the law‚Äîit's 2-3x of the perceived value.
