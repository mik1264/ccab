üß† Why It Works: Cognitive Dissonance

The logic: "I did something nice for this person. I wouldn't help someone I dislike. Therefore, I must like them."

Self-perception theory: We infer our attitudes from our behaviors. Helping = evidence of liking.

Investment effect: We value what we invest in. Helping someone is an investment‚Äîso we value the relationship more.

The Science

Named after: Benjamin Franklin, who used this technique deliberately in 1736 to win over a hostile Pennsylvania legislator.

The paradox: Common sense says receiving favors creates gratitude and liking. But the GIVER of the favor actually develops MORE positive feelings than the receiver!

Application: To win someone over, don't shower them with favors‚Äîask them for a small, easy favor. Borrow a book. Ask for advice. Request a small help. They'll like you more for it.

The best way to make a friend is not to do them a favor‚Äîbut to let them do you one.
