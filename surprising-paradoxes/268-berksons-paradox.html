What's Happening?

Berkson's Paradox (1946) reveals how selection based on a "collider" creates spurious correlations that don't exist in the full population. When you only observe a subset selected by a criterion involving two variables, those variables appear negatively correlated‚Äîeven if they're completely independent or positively correlated in reality!

In the dating pool: If you only date people who are at least attractive OR nice (a minimum standard), then among your dates, these traits will appear negatively correlated. Why? The nice-but-plain people in your pool "made the cut" via niceness, while the pretty-but-rude ones made it via looks. The corner with low-both gets excluded!
