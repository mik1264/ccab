üß† Resolving the Paradox

The flaw: The argument treats D as a fixed value, but it's actually a random variable that means different things in different scenarios!

When your envelope has the SMALLER amount:
D = X (smaller), other = 2X ‚Üí gain X by switching

When your envelope has the LARGER amount:
D = 2X (larger), other = X ‚Üí lose X by switching

The "D" in each case represents DIFFERENT distributions!

Correct calculation: Let X = the smaller amount (fixed). Your envelope is equally likely to contain X or 2X.

E(your envelope) = ¬Ω(X) + ¬Ω(2X) = 1.5X
E(other envelope) = ¬Ω(2X) + ¬Ω(X) = 1.5X

Both expectations are EQUAL! No advantage to switching.

The insight: You cannot simultaneously condition on "my envelope has D" AND treat the other envelope as equally likely to be 2D or D/2. Once you know your amount, the probabilities change! This is a failure to properly apply Bayes' theorem.
