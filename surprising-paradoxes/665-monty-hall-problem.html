Why Our Intuition Fails

The Equiprobability Bias: We assume the two remaining doors must have equal probability (50/50). But the host's deliberate choice to reveal a goat changes everything!

Your initial choice: 1/3 chance of being right.

The other two doors combined: 2/3 chance of having the car.

After the host opens one: That entire 2/3 probability collapses onto the remaining door!

Think of it this way: "Would you trade your 1 door for BOTH other doors?" Of course! That's effectively what switching does.
