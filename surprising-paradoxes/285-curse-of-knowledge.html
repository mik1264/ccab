üß† Why Knowledge Curses Us

Simulation failure: We can't accurately simulate our own prior ignorance. Knowledge changes how we process information, and we can't undo that change.

Anchoring on ourselves: We use our own understanding as the baseline and fail to adjust enough for others' different knowledge states.

The melody problem: When you tap a song, you HEAR the melody in your head. It seems inseparable from the taps. But listeners only hear taps‚Äîno melody accompanies them.

The Science

Coined by: Economists Colin Camerer, George Loewenstein, and Martin Weber in 1989, inspired by cognitive psychology research. Elizabeth Newton's tapping study (1990) became the iconic demonstration.

The paradox: The more expert you become, the WORSE you get at explaining to beginners. Your knowledge becomes "obvious" and you lose access to the beginner's confusion.

Defense: Test your explanations on actual beginners. Watch their faces. Ask what they DON'T understand. Use analogies, examples, and concrete language. Assume less shared knowledge than feels natural.

The expert's greatest blindspot is forgetting they were once blind.
