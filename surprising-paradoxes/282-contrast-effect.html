The Paradox

We don't perceive things as they truly are‚Äîwe perceive them relative to what came before. A $50 shirt seems cheap after seeing a $500 jacket. A candidate seems weak after a stellar interviewee. Room-temperature water feels cold after hot water, warm after cold water. The paradox: the same thing can seem completely different depending on context.

üõ°Ô∏è Reducing Contrast Bias

  • üìè

    Use Absolute Standards

    Define criteria before evaluating. Rate against fixed benchmarks, not against other options you've just seen.

  • ‚è∞

    Add Time Between Comparisons

    The contrast effect weakens with time. Wait before making decisions after seeing something extreme.

  • üîÄ

    Randomize Order

    When evaluating multiple options (candidates, products), randomize the order to prevent systematic bias.

  • üìù

    Evaluate Independently First

    Form your opinion of each option before comparing them directly. Write down your assessment before seeing alternatives.
