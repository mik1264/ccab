The Paradox

You feel your nervousness radiating outward. Your lie must be written on your face. Your disgust is surely obvious to everyone. But here's the paradox: others can't see nearly as much as you think they can. We're anchored to our own intense inner experience and assume it "leaks out"‚Äîbut to observers, our face often reveals nothing. Your thoughts feel transparent. They're not.
