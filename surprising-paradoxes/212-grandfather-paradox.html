Proposed Resolutions

1. The Novikov Self-Consistency Principle

Time travel is possible, but paradoxes are not. Any attempt to change the past will be thwarted by probability itself. The gun jams, you slip on a banana peel, your grandfather happens to step away. Whatever happened, happened. The timeline is self-consistent by necessity.

2. Many Worlds Interpretation

You don't change YOUR past‚Äîyou create a new branch of reality. In this new timeline, your grandfather dies and "you" are never born. But the original timeline continues unchanged‚Äîyou still exist in it. Parallel universes resolve the paradox.

3. Time Travel is Impossible

The simplest resolution: backward time travel to the past is fundamentally impossible. While relativity allows time dilation (traveling to the future slowly), it may not permit closed timelike curves. The paradox proves backward time travel cannot exist.

4. The Chronology Protection Conjecture

Stephen Hawking proposed that the laws of physics conspire to prevent time travel paradoxes. Any attempt to build a time machine would be destroyed by quantum effects before it could create a paradox. Nature protects chronology.
