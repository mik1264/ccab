When Groups Do What Nobody Wants

The Paradox

A group unanimously agrees to take an action that no individual member actually wants. Each person privately disagrees, but assumes everyone else is in favor‚Äîso they go along to avoid conflict.

The result? A decision that makes everyone unhappy, made by a group where no one wanted it. The problem isn't disagreement‚Äîit's the failure to voice disagreement.
