The Shocking Answer: 50 Pounds!

Wait... WHAT?!

Yes, you read that right. By going from 99% water to 98% water‚Äîa mere 1 percentage point difference‚Äîthe potatoes lose HALF their weight!

The key insight is that the solid matter stays constant. Only water evaporates. So we need to figure out what total weight would make 1 pound of solid equal to 2% of the whole.

Initial State 100 lbs total = 99 lbs water + 1 lb solid
Solid is what percent? 1 / 100 = 1%
After drying, solid must be 100% - 98% = 2%
If 1 lb = 2% of total, then total = 1 / 0.02 = 50 lbs
Water in final state 50 - 1 = 49 lbs (98% of 50)

The Key Formula

Final Weight = Solid Mass √∑ Solid Percentage
50 = 1 √∑ 0.02

The paradox tricks us because we focus on the water percentage changing by 1%, when what really matters is that the solid percentage DOUBLED (from 1% to 2%). For the solid to go from 1% to 2% of the total, the total must halve!

The General Pattern

This paradox works for any high water content. If something is:

‚Ä¢ 99.9% water ‚Üí 99.8% water: loses 50% of weight

The closer to 100% water, the more dramatic the effect!

Why Our Intuition Fails

We intuitively think: "1% less water means losing about 1 lb."

But percentages are relative, not absolute! When nearly everything is water, the tiny solid fraction becomes the anchor point. The denominator (total weight) must shrink dramatically to double that small percentage.
