The Potato Paradox

100 lbs of potatoes are 99% water. After drying to 98% water, how much do they weigh? Most people guess wrong by 50 pounds!

50 lbs

The Puzzle

Fred brings home 100 pounds of potatoes from the market. These potatoes are 99% water by weight.

He leaves them outside overnight. Some water evaporates, and now the potatoes are 98% water.

How much do the potatoes weigh now?

🤔 What's Your Guess?

Most people estimate around 98 or 99 pounds. After all, only 1% of water was lost, right?

The actual answer: 50 pounds!

Why 50 Pounds?

The key insight: the solid matter doesn't change. Only water evaporates.

Before:
Total = 100 lbs
Water = 99% × 100 = 99 lbs
Solids = 1% × 100 = 1 lb

After:
Solids still = 1 lb (unchanged!)
Solids = 2% of new total
1 lb = 2% × Total
Total = 1 / 0.02 = 50 lbs

To go from 1% solids to 2% solids, the potatoes must lose half their total weight. That means 50 pounds of water evaporated!

Why Our Intuition Fails

We intuitively think: "1% less water = roughly 1% less weight." But this ignores a crucial point:

💡 The Percentage Trap

When something is 99% X, even a small change in that percentage represents a HUGE absolute change, because the base amount of X is so large.

Going from 99% to 98% water means the non-water portion doubles (from 1% to 2%). For that to happen without adding solids, the total must halve.

Another way to see it:

  • At 99% water: 1 lb solids is 1/100 of total
  • At 98% water: 1 lb solids must be 1/50 of total
  • So total must be 50 lbs

The General Pattern

For any starting and ending water percentage:

Let S = solid weight (constant)
Let W1 = initial water %, W2 = final water %
Initial total = S / (1 - W1)
Final total = S / (1 - W2)
Ratio = (1 - W1) / (1 - W2)

For our potato case: (1 - 0.99) / (1 - 0.98) = 0.01 / 0.02 = 0.5

The final weight is 50% of the original!

99% 0% 1 lb (just solids)

Real-World Implications

This paradox has practical applications:

  • Food Processing: Drying foods dramatically reduces weight. A 99% water vegetable dried to 90% water loses 90% of its mass!
  • Shipping Costs: It's often cheaper to ship concentrated products and add water locally.
  • Nutrition Labels: "Per serving" values can be misleading when comparing fresh vs. dried foods.
  • Medical: Dehydration effects on body weight. A person losing "just" 2% body water (going from ~60% to ~58%) has lost significant fluid.

🍅 Fruit Example

Fresh grapes are ~80% water. Raisins are ~15% water. That's why it takes about 4 lbs of grapes to make 1 lb of raisins!

The Deeper Lesson

The potato paradox teaches us that percentages near 100% are deceptively sensitive. A small percentage change near an extreme represents a large absolute change.

This applies to:

  • Purity: Going from 99.9% to 99.99% pure means removing 90% of remaining impurities
  • Reliability: "Five nines" (99.999%) uptime is 100x better than "three nines" (99.9%)
  • Completion: The last 1% of a project often takes as long as the first 99%

Our brains evolved for mid-range percentages. At the extremes, linear intuition fails.
