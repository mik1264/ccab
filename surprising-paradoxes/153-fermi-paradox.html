The Fermi Paradox is the apparent contradiction between the high probability of extraterrestrial civilizations existing in the universe and the complete lack of evidence for‚Äîor contact with‚Äîsuch civilizations.

Given that our Sun is a relatively young star, there should have been ample time for intelligent life to develop elsewhere and spread across the galaxy. Even at sub-light speeds, a civilization could colonize the entire Milky Way in a few million years‚Äîa blink of an eye on cosmic timescales.

So where is everybody?

Why It Matters

The Fermi Paradox isn't just about aliens‚Äîit's about our own future. If the Great Filter lies ahead, finding microbial life on Mars would be terrifying news: it would mean the filter isn't the origin of life, so it must be something we haven't faced yet.

Conversely, if we're truly alone, we bear an immense responsibility. We might be the only consciousness in a galaxy of 400 billion stars‚Äîthe universe's only way of knowing itself.

Either answer is profound. Either we're surrounded by silent civilizations hiding from some cosmic danger, or we're the only light in an unimaginably vast darkness.
