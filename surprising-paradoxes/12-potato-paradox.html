The Potato Paradox

How losing 1% water makes you lose half your weight

The Puzzle

You have 100 pounds of potatoes that are 99% water. You leave them out to dry until they're 98% water. How much do the potatoes weigh now?

Most people guess 99 pounds or 98 pounds. After all, you only lost 1% of water... right?

Wrong. The potatoes now weigh only 50 pounds. Losing just 1% water content means losing half the total weight!

The Mathematics

The key insight is that the solid portion never changes. Only water evaporates.

Initial: 100 lbs total, 99% water = 99 lbs water + 1 lb solids
After drying: Solids still = 1 lb
If final is 98% water, then solids = 2%
1 lb = 2% of final weight
Final weight = 1 √∑ 0.02 = 50 lbs

The general formula:

Final Weight = Initial Solids √∑ (1 - Final Water %)
= Initial Weight √ó (1 - Initial Water %) √∑ (1 - Final Water %)

Why It's Counterintuitive

Our brains focus on the percentage change (99% ‚Üí 98% = losing 1%) rather than the ratio change.

  • At 99% water, solids are 1% of total weight
  • At 98% water, solids are 2% of total weight
  • The solid percentage doubled!
  • Since solid weight is fixed, total weight must halve

The closer the water percentage is to 100%, the more dramatic the effect. Going from 99.9% to 99.8% water would cause a 50% weight loss too!

The General Pattern

This paradox illustrates a broader mathematical truth about ratios near extremes:

When something is 99% one thing and 1% another, even a tiny change in percentage points causes massive changes in the ratio‚Äîand thus in any quantity that depends on that ratio.

The same principle applies to:

  • Gold purity (24k to 23k is a bigger relative change than 12k to 11k)
  • Test accuracy (99% to 98% doubles false positive rate)
  • Reliability engineering (99.9% to 99.8% uptime doubles downtime)
