The Potato Paradox

A 1% change in water content causes a 50% loss in weight. How?!

99% WATER → 98% WATER = HALF THE WEIGHT?!

You have 100 kg of potatoes that are 99% water. After drying overnight, they're 98% water. What do they weigh now? Most people guess wrong!

The Key Insight

The solids (1 kg) stay constant! For 1 kg to go from 1% to 2% of the total, the total must HALVE.

Why Our Intuition Fails

We see "99% → 98%" and think "1% change, so about 1% weight loss." But that's wrong! We're confusing absolute and relative changes.

The water percentage dropped by 1 percentage point (from 99% to 98%), but the solid percentage doubled (from 1% to 2%). Since the solid mass is fixed, doubling its percentage means halving the total!

Final Weight = Solid Mass ÷ (1 - Final Water %)
The smaller the solid percentage, the more dramatic the effect!

Real-world implications: This paradox appears in dehydration calculations, concentration problems, and any scenario involving percentages of a changing total. It's why "97% fat-free" to "94% fat-free" isn't a small change—it's doubling the fat content!
