üß† Why Virtue Enables Vice

Moral credentials: Good deeds build up credits in a mental "moral bank account." Bad deeds feel like spending down the balance, not personality change.

Goal substitution: The goal of APPEARING moral gets satisfied by good deeds, reducing motivation for actual moral behavior. The performance replaces the substance.

Self-licensing: "I'm clearly a good person (look at what I did!), so this minor transgression doesn't define me." Past virtue provides cover for present vice.
