Why Does This Happen?

Most people guess you'd need around 180 people for a 50% chance of a shared birthday. The actual answer‚Äîjust 23‚Äîseems impossibly low.

The Confusion: We instinctively think "What's the chance someone shares my birthday?" (about 6% with 23 people). But the question asks about any pair matching‚Äîa completely different calculation with 253 possible pairs!

Counterintuitive Because...

Our brains fail at this problem for two reasons:

  1. We think linearly: We expect 50% probability to need ~182 people (half of 365)
  2. We're egocentric: We calculate "match with ME" instead of "match with ANYONE"

The number of pairs grows quadratically: n people create n(n-1)/2 pairs. With 23 people, that's 253 pairs‚Äîeach an independent chance for a match!
