üí° The Key Insight

We compare PAIRS, not individuals! With 23 people, there are 253 pairs to check. Each pair has a 1/365 chance of matching‚Äîbut with 253 tries, matches become likely!

üßÆ The Mathematics

Instead of calculating matches, we calculate NO matches:

P(no match) = 365/365 √ó 364/365 √ó 363/365 √ó ...

P(match) = 1 - P(no match)

Each new person must avoid ALL previous birthdays. The probability shrinks fast!

ü§î Why Our Intuition Fails

We instinctively think: "What's the chance someone shares MY birthday?" That's 1/365 per person.

But the question is: "Do ANY two people match?" This involves comparing every pair‚Äîand pairs grow quadratically!

Pairs = n(n-1)/2
23 people ‚Üí 253 pairs!

üîê The Birthday Attack

Cryptographers use this principle! A birthday attack finds hash collisions much faster than brute force.

For a hash with 2^n possible values, you only need ~2^(n/2) attempts to find a collision with 50% probability.

This is why cryptographic hashes need to be MUCH longer than you'd naively expect!

üéØ The Pigeonhole Principle

With 367 people, a match is GUARANTEED (there are only 366 possible birthdays including Feb 29).

But the birthday paradox shows we reach near-certainty MUCH sooner‚Äî99.9% at just 70 people!
