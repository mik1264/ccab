Benford's Law

The first digit of natural data is NOT uniformly distributed! The digit "1" appears ~30% of the time, while "9" appears only ~5%. This is used to detect fraud in financial records!

Why Does This Happen?

The Logarithmic Scale Explanation

Think about how numbers grow. To go from 1 to 2, you double (100% increase). To go from 5 to 6, you only increase by 20%. Numbers "spend more time" with lower first digits because it takes longer (in multiplicative terms) to transition away from them.

When data spans multiple orders of magnitude (like populations ranging from thousands to billions), the logarithms of these numbers become uniformly distributed ‚Äî and Benford's Law emerges naturally!

Historical Discovery

Simon Newcomb (1881) noticed that the early pages of logarithm tables were more worn than later pages ‚Äî suggesting people looked up numbers starting with 1 more often!

Frank Benford (1938) tested 20,000+ numbers from various sources and formalized the law.

Modern Use: Benford's Law is admissible in US courts for fraud detection. It exposed Bernie Madoff's Ponzi scheme, irregularities in Greece's EU application, and countless tax fraud cases.
