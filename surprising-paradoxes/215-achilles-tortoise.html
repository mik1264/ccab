Zeno's Argument (circa 450 BCE)

Before Achilles can catch the tortoise, he must first reach where the tortoise IS NOW. But by the time he gets there, the tortoise has moved further ahead. Achilles must then run to THAT new position, but again, the tortoise has moved. This process continues forever!

The Mathematical Resolution

Zeno's paradox assumes that infinite steps require infinite time. But they don't! The sum of an infinite geometric series can be FINITE:

10 + 1 + 0.1 + 0.01 + 0.001 + ... = 10 √ó (1/(1-0.1)) = 10 √ó (10/9) = 11.111... meters

Achilles catches the tortoise at exactly 11.111... meters in 1.111... seconds. The infinite sum CONVERGES!

Why It Still Matters

Zeno's paradoxes predate calculus by 2,000 years. They challenged ancient Greeks to think rigorously about infinity, continuity, and motion. The resolution required the development of mathematical limits and convergent series‚Äîconcepts that wouldn't be formalized until Newton and Leibniz invented calculus in the 17th century.

The deeper question remains: Is space infinitely divisible, or is there a smallest unit (like the Planck length)? Does motion really pass through infinite intermediate positions, or does nature work differently at the quantum level?
