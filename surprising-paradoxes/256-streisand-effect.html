üß† Why It Happens

Psychological reactance: When freedom is threatened, people push back. Telling someone they CAN'T see something makes them WANT to see it. The censorship attempt signals that the content must be valuable or threatening‚Äîotherwise, why suppress it?

The Mechanism

1. The Attempt: Someone tries to remove, hide, or censor information from the internet.

2. The Story: The suppression attempt ITSELF becomes news. "They're trying to hide something!" is more interesting than the original content.

3. The Spread: People share the content specifically BECAUSE it's being suppressed. Copies multiply faster than they can be removed.

4. The Permanence: The content becomes associated with the suppression attempt forever. The Streisand Effect becomes the legacy.

Named by Mike Masnick of Techdirt in 2005. Now a standard term in internet culture and media studies.
