Wave Interference Theory

Wave Equation: ∂²u/∂t² = c² ∇²u

When two or more waves overlap, they interfere with each other:

  • Constructive Interference: Waves in phase add up (bright regions)
  • Destructive Interference: Waves out of phase cancel (dark regions)
  • Nodes: Points of zero displacement (permanent dark spots)
  • Antinodes: Points of maximum displacement (bright oscillating spots)

Applications: Noise cancellation, diffraction gratings, interferometers, holography, and Young's double-slit experiment.
