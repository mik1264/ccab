Diffusion-Limited Aggregation (DLA)

A fractal growth process where particles undergo random walks until they stick to a growing cluster. This creates beautiful branching patterns found in nature: lightning, crystals, coral, and more.

How it works:
Particles are released from a circle and random walk until they touch the aggregate. Lower sticking probability creates more delicate branches. The fractal dimension measures how the structure fills space (typically ~1.7 for 2D DLA).
