Page's Complex Adaptive Systems

42 paired simulations from Scott E. Page's Model Thinking — Agent-Based Models and System Dynamics for the same families of problems.

21 ABM · 21 SD · 42 total
Two lenses on emergence. Scott E. Page argues that complex adaptive systems are best understood by deploying many models. Agent-based models grow macro patterns from heterogeneous agents and local rules — useful when discreteness, networks, and individual differences matter. System dynamics use stocks, flows, and feedback to make aggregate behaviour legible — useful when continuous quantities, delays, and reinforcing loops dominate. Both lenses appear repeatedly in Page's curated examples: Schelling tipping, beehive thresholds, sand piles, predator-prey, epidemics, growth, and diffusion. Pair them and the difference between micro mechanism and macro structure becomes visible.
