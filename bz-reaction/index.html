About the Belousov-Zhabotinsky Reaction

The BZ reaction is one of the most famous examples of non-equilibrium chemical oscillation. When certain chemicals (malonic acid, potassium bromate, and cerium or ferroin catalyst) are mixed, they produce stunning visual patterns including rotating spirals and concentric waves.

This simulation uses the Oregonator model, a simplified mathematical description of the BZ reaction based on reaction-diffusion equations.

Target Patterns (Pacemakers)

In the BZ reaction, certain regions can act as pacemaker centers, spontaneously emitting concentric circular waves. These target patterns emerge when oscillations occur at different frequencies across the medium.

  • Each pacemaker has a characteristic frequency
  • Faster pacemakers dominate slower ones
  • Multiple targets create complex interference patterns
