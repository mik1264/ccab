EFT_asym breakdown

Wind intensity vs latitude

Peak at ~45° (the jet stream) and a smaller peak near ~75°. The PDF models this as a signed coefficient: positive near the equator (trade winds oppose eastbound flights), negative in mid-latitudes (westerlies help them).

How to read this

The Njord formula uses a single number — EFT_asym — to capture the net wind effect along the great-circle route. It depends on Δlongitude, the latitude of the route, and whether the path climbs above both airports into the jet-stream band. The correction is divided by V², so slow aircraft feel wind effects more strongly. Flight times shown are estimates from method 3 of the PDF (no empirical data blend).
