Reading Ice Cores

Ice cores preserve annual layers of compressed snow. Trapped air bubbles record atmospheric composition. Isotope ratios (δ18O) reveal past temperatures. Volcanic eruptions leave sulfate spikes. Dust layers indicate arid, windy conditions. The deepest Antarctic cores reach 800,000 years back.
