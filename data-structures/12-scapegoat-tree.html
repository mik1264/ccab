How Scapegoat Trees Work

A Scapegoat Tree maintains balance by periodically rebuilding subtrees rather than using rotations. When a node becomes too deep, we find a "scapegoat" ancestor and rebuild its entire subtree.

Balance condition: size(child) ≤ α × size(node)

Rebuild trigger: When depth > log1/α(n), find scapegoat where size(node) > α × size(parent) and rebuild.
