Schickard's Calculating Clock (1623)

Wilhelm Schickard, a professor at Tubingen, designed the first known mechanical calculator in 1623 for his friend Johannes Kepler. It combined a set of Napier's bones for multiplication in the upper section with a six-digit gear-driven adding mechanism below. The single surviving prototype was destroyed in a fire; modern reconstructions are based on Schickard's letters and sketches.
