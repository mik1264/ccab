Ancient Writing Systems

From Clay to Code — The Evolution of Written Language

Writing is humanity’s most transformative invention. From Sumerian cuneiform pressed into wet clay to the mysterious Rongorongo glyphs of Easter Island, each script tells the story of a civilization’s need to record, communicate, and transcend time. Explore 10 interactive simulations spanning 5,000 years of written language—stamp wedge marks, encode your name in hieroglyphics, convert numbers to Mayan vigesimal, cast Elder Futhark runes, and examine undeciphered scripts that still defy modern scholars.

Mesopotamia & Egypt

The cradle of writing: cuneiform and hieroglyphics, the two oldest fully-developed writing systems, born along the great river valleys.

Cuneiform Tablet

Rosetta Stone Trilingual Comparison

Elder Futhark Runes

Mayan Number System

Convert any number into the Mayan vigesimal (base-20) system using dots (1), bars (5), and shells (0). Watch a counting animation or enter numbers up to 999,999.

Oracle Bone Script

Scratch characters onto a virtual ox scapula as Shang Dynasty diviners did (c. 1200 BCE). Stamp pictographic signs for sun, moon, rain, and fire, then generate heat cracks for fortune-telling.
