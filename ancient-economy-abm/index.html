About This Model

What is AE-ABM?

Ancient Economy Agent-Based Model (AE-ABM) is a NetLogo-style simulation designed to explore three competing theories of the Roman economy:

  • Market Integration (Temin): Well-integrated markets under Pax Romana
  • Embedded Economy (Polanyi/Finley): Status and institutions dominate exchange
  • Tributary/Bazaar (Bang): Long-distance trade within a tribute-extracting empire

Key Features

  • Agent Types: Households/Farms, Traders, Temple/State institutions, Mint/Treasury
  • Commodities: Grain (production, storage, trade) and Silver coin (money supply)
  • Transport: Sea, river, and road networks with realistic cost ratios (1:5:34-42)
  • Time: Seasonal ticks with planting/harvest/travel windows
  • Data: Calibrated to ORBIS network, Pleiades places, and archaeological price evidence

Milestones

M0 (Current): Basic grid world, local auctions, friction sliders
M1: ORBIS network, sea/river/road edges, seasonal navigation
M2: Institutional modes, taxation, redistribution
M3: Policy shocks, price edicts, coin supply dynamics
M4: Calibration and validation against historical data
