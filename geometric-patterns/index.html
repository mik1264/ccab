6 Mathematical and Cultural Pattern Explorations

Explore the intersection of mathematics, culture, and art through timeless geometric patterns. From Islamic tessellations to Celtic knots, from Penrose tilings to sacred geometry, these patterns demonstrate the universal language of shape, symmetry, and proportion found across civilizations and throughout history.

Cultural Geometric Traditions

Geometric patterns from ancient cultural traditions around the world, showcasing mathematical precision and artistic beauty.

Islamic Geometric Art

Traditional Islamic patterns with 8-point and 12-point stars, hexagonal tessellations, and square Kufic designs. Features the intricate mathematical principles found in mosque architecture.

Sacred Geometry

Ancient patterns including Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube, Sri Yantra, and Vesica Piscis. Explore forms believed to represent fundamental patterns of creation.

Celtic Knots

Traditional Celtic interlaced patterns with endless knots, triquetra, and woven designs. Features the distinctive over-under weaving symbolizing eternity.

Mathematical Tiling & Tessellations

Patterns based on advanced mathematical principles including aperiodic tiling and computational geometry.

Penrose Tiling

Aperiodic tiling with fivefold symmetry discovered by Roger Penrose. These patterns never repeat but tile infinitely using kites and darts in golden ratio proportions.

Truchet Tiles

Simple tiles rotated randomly create complex emergent patterns. Named after 18th-century mathematician Sébastien Truchet, generating unique maze-like structures.

Voronoi Diagram Art

Beautiful colored Voronoi cells where each point owns the region closest to it. Watch the dynamic space partitioning with animated points. Click to add more!
