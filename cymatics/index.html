Cymatics

Sound Made Visible: How vibrations create stunning geometric patterns

Cymatics — from the Greek kyma (wave) — is the study of visible sound. When a surface vibrates at specific frequencies, it organizes particles, liquids, and even fire into astonishingly precise geometric patterns. These patterns aren't designed; they emerge spontaneously from the physics of resonance.

Ernst Chladni first revealed this in 1787 by drawing a violin bow across sand-covered metal plates. The sand collected along the nodal lines — places where the plate doesn't move — creating intricate figures that change with each frequency. These simulations let you explore the hidden geometry of sound.
