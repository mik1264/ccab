Jacquard Loom (1804)

Joseph Marie Jacquard revolutionized textile weaving with punched cards that controlled which warp threads were raised.

Each row of holes on a card corresponds to one row of the woven pattern. A hole means the thread is raised; no hole means it stays lowered.

This concept directly inspired Charles Babbage and later, Herman Hollerith's census machines — the ancestors of modern computing.
