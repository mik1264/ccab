Public Goods Game

Free-rider problem and cooperation dynamics

Public Goods Game: Each player has $20 per round. They can contribute any amount to a public pool. The pool is multiplied by a factor and split equally among all players. Rational self-interest leads to zero contributions, but mutual cooperation yields higher total payoffs. This demonstrates the free-rider problem.
