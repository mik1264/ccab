About the Model

The ethnocentrism model reveals how cooperation boundaries emerge from simple rules: agents have inheritable color tags and two genes controlling cooperation with same-color and different-color neighbors. Through spatial Prisoner's Dilemma interactions, ethnocentric agents (cooperate with same only) form self-reinforcing clusters that dominate the population. This demonstrates how tribalism and ethnic conflict can arise from evolutionary game dynamics rather than innate prejudice, with profound implications for understanding human social behavior.
