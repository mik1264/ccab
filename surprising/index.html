Prepare to Question Everything

These aren't just mathematical curiosities - they're fundamental truths about probability, statistics, and decision-making that affect your daily life. From medical testing to traffic planning, from social networks to game shows, these paradoxes reveal why our intuitions often fail us.

Each visualization is interactive, allowing you to experiment with parameters and see the surprising results for yourself. The best way to truly understand these phenomena is to explore them hands-on.

The Birthday Paradox

How many people do you need for a 50% chance of a shared birthday? The answer will surprise you: just 23! Watch the probability climb as you add people to the room.

Probability 02

The Monty Hall Problem

Should you switch doors? This probability puzzle stumped even mathematicians. Play the game yourself and watch the statistics prove that switching doubles your odds.

Game Theory 03

Simpson's Paradox

A trend appears in groups but reverses when combined. See how UC Berkeley was sued for gender bias despite favoring women in every department.

Statistics 04

The Friendship Paradox

Your friends have more friends than you do - on average. This isn't low self-esteem, it's mathematics! Explore an interactive network to see why.

Networks 05

Braess's Paradox

Adding a road can make traffic WORSE for everyone. This counterintuitive result has led to actual road closures that improved traffic flow.

The False Positive Paradox

A 99% accurate test can be wrong most of the time. Understanding this is crucial for interpreting medical tests, security screening, and more.
