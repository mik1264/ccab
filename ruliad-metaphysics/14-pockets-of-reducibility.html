Pockets of Reducibility

Even in computationally irreducible systems, small regions remain locally predictable: periodic backgrounds, gliders, and conserved structures act as pockets of reducibility that simple summary laws can describe. Science finds its "laws of nature" precisely by inhabiting these pockets. Toggle between Rule 110, Rule 54 (highly reducible) and Rule 30 (almost pure chaos) to see how rare these islands of order can be.
