Universal Substrates of the Ruliad

The ruliad is the entangled limit of all possible computations. Inside it live countless computationally-universal systems — radically different rules that nonetheless can each compute anything the others can. Below are six known universal substrates running in parallel, illustrating Wolfram's Principle of Computational Equivalence.

All Substrates
All six are computationally equivalent — they all compute the same things, despite radically different rules.
