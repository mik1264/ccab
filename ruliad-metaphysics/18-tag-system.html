Tag System

A tag system reads the first symbol of a string, looks up its production, deletes the first m symbols, and appends the production to the tail. With as few as two symbols and three productions (m=2), tag systems are universal — capable of simulating any Turing machine. They show how universal computation lives inside remarkably minimal rule sets, a key motif of the ruliad.
