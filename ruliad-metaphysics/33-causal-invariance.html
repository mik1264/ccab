Causal Invariance / Confluence

For our universe to possess a single objective causal structure — and therefore consistent relativity — the underlying rewrite rules must be causally invariant: no matter which order we apply them, all branches of the multiway system eventually merge back together. Below, the left system is confluent (paths reunite at abc); the right system is not (paths diverge into separate equivalence classes forever).

In Wolfram's framework, causal invariance is why our universe has a single causal structure. Different observers may choose different "foliations" of the multiway graph (different orderings of rewrite events), yet they all agree on the same sequence of cause-and-effect relationships — precisely because confluent rules guarantee the branches reunite. This emergent agreement is special relativity at the level of the ruliad. Without confluence (right panel), there would be no consistent classical history at all.
