Time's Arrow from Coarse-Graining

Underneath, the rule is a reversible block cellular automaton (Margolus neighborhood): every microstate has a unique predecessor, so fine-grained entropy is exactly constant. The arrow of time you see — a small clump dispersing into uniform haze — is purely an artifact of a computationally-bounded observer who can only see block-averaged density. Press Reverse and the microstate retraces itself; press Perturb to flip a single cell and watch reversibility shatter.

After Wolfram: the Second Law is not a law of physics, it is a law of observers.
