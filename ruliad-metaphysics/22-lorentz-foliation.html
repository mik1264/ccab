Relativity from Causal Foliations

In Wolfram's framework, special relativity is not fundamental — it emerges because different observers correspond to different foliations of the same underlying causal graph. A Lorentz boost is simply a tilt of the foliation lines. Drag either observer's tilt: spacelike-separated events swap order between observers (relativity of simultaneity) while the causal graph itself stays invariant.
