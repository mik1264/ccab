About Optical Illusions

Optical illusions demonstrate that what we "see" is not just a direct representation of reality, but rather a construction created by our brain. These phenomena occur because our visual system uses shortcuts, assumptions, and contextual information to interpret the world around us.

Why do optical illusions work?

  • Neural Processing: Our brains use edge detection, contrast, and motion to make sense of visual input
  • Contextual Interpretation: Surrounding elements influence how we perceive individual components
  • Evolutionary Shortcuts: Our visual system evolved to quickly interpret 3D scenes, leading to systematic "errors"
  • Peripheral Vision: Different parts of our visual field process information differently
  • Temporal Processing: Motion and change detection can override static pattern recognition
