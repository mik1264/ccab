Reversible Enzyme Inhibition

Enzyme inhibitors are molecules that reduce or block enzyme activity. Reversible inhibitors bind non-covalently and can be removed to restore activity.

Types of Inhibition

Competitive Inhibition:

  • Inhibitor binds to the active site, competing with substrate
  • Increases apparent Km (lower affinity)
  • Vmax unchanged (can be overcome with high [S])
  • Lineweaver-Burk: Lines intersect at y-axis (same 1/Vmax)

Noncompetitive Inhibition:

  • Inhibitor binds to allosteric site on both E and ES
  • Km unchanged (binding doesn't affect affinity)
  • Vmax decreased (fewer functional enzymes)
  • Lineweaver-Burk: Lines intersect at x-axis (same -1/Km)

Uncompetitive Inhibition:

  • Inhibitor binds only to ES complex
  • Both Km and Vmax decrease proportionally
  • Cannot be overcome with high [S]
  • Lineweaver-Burk: Parallel lines (same slope)

The Lineweaver-Burk Plot

A double-reciprocal plot (1/v vs 1/[S]) that linearizes Michaelis-Menten kinetics:

  • Y-intercept = 1/Vmax
  • X-intercept = -1/Km
  • Slope = Km/Vmax
