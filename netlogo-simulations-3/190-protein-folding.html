The HP Lattice Model

The HP model is a simplified representation of protein folding where amino acids are classified as either Hydrophobic (H) or Polar (P). The protein folds on a 2D or 3D lattice.

The Folding Problem

Given a sequence of amino acids, how does a protein find its unique native structure? This is one of biology's grand challenges:

  • Levinthal's Paradox: Random search would take longer than the age of the universe
  • Funnel Model: The energy landscape guides folding toward the native state
  • Thermodynamic Hypothesis: The native state is the global free energy minimum

Energy Function

In the HP model, energy comes from H-H contacts:

  • Each non-sequential H-H contact contributes -1 to energy
  • Lower (more negative) energy = better fold
  • Goal: Maximize H-H contacts (hydrophobic collapse)

Monte Carlo Moves

  • Corner flip: Move a residue at a bend to the opposite corner
  • End move: Rotate chain ends to adjacent empty positions
  • Crankshaft: Rotate a segment of the chain
