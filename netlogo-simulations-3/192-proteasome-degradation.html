Proteasome Degradation

The 26S proteasome is a 2.4 MDa molecular machine that degrades over 80% of cellular proteins. It's the central executor of the ubiquitin-proteasome system (UPS).

Ubiquitination Cascade

  • E1 (Activating): Activates ubiquitin using ATP
  • E2 (Conjugating): Carries activated ubiquitin
  • E3 (Ligase): Recognizes target proteins and transfers ubiquitin
  • Polyubiquitin chain (‚â•4 Ub) marks proteins for degradation

26S Proteasome Structure

  • 19S Regulatory Particle: Recognizes ubiquitin chains (Rpn1/10/13), deubiquitinates (Rpn11), unfolds substrates (Rpt1-6 ATPases)
  • 20S Core Particle: Contains proteolytic chamber (Œ≤1, Œ≤2, Œ≤5 peptidases)
  • Processing: ~50 kDa protein / ~50 seconds / ~80 ATP molecules

Quality Control

Misfolded, damaged, or regulatory proteins are targeted for degradation. The UPS controls:

  • Cell cycle progression (cyclins)
  • Transcription factors (NF-Œ∫B, p53)
  • Protein quality control
  • Immune response (MHC peptides)

Key Concepts

  • Enzyme Kinetics: Enzymes catalyze reactions following Michaelis-Menten kinetics, with rates determined by substrate concentration and enzyme affinity (Km).
  • Lock and Key vs Induced Fit: Enzymes recognize substrates either through rigid complementarity (lock-key) or conformational changes upon binding (induced fit).
  • Allosteric Regulation: Enzyme activity modulated by molecules binding at sites other than the active site, enabling sophisticated metabolic control.
  • Signal Transduction: Cascades of molecular interactions that amplify and transmit signals from cell surface to nucleus.
