Competitor Detection

Bacteria can detect the presence of competitors through various signals: cell damage products, competitor-specific molecules, or resource depletion patterns. Upon detection, they may activate defensive behaviors like toxin production, increased motility to escape, biofilm formation for protection, or resource hoarding.
