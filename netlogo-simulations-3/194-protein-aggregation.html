Amyloid Protein Aggregation

Amyloid fibrils are ordered protein aggregates with a characteristic cross-Œ≤ structure. Their formation underlies diseases like Alzheimer's (AŒ≤), Parkinson's (Œ±-synuclein), and Huntington's.

Nucleation-Polymerization Mechanism

  • Primary Nucleation: Rare, rate-limiting formation of initial seeds from monomers
  • Secondary Nucleation: New nuclei form on fibril surfaces (catalytic autocatalysis)
  • Elongation: Monomers add to fibril ends via induced fit
  • Fragmentation: Fibrils break, creating more growth-competent ends

Kinetic Phases

  • Lag Phase: Few aggregates; primary nucleation dominant
  • Growth Phase: Exponential increase; secondary nucleation drives rapid fibril multiplication
  • Plateau Phase: Monomer depletion; equilibrium reached

ThT Fluorescence

Thioflavin T (ThT) is a dye that fluoresces when bound to cross-Œ≤ structure. The sigmoidal ThT curve is a hallmark of amyloid kinetics.

Toxic Oligomers

Small oligomeric intermediates are often more toxic than mature fibrils. They can disrupt membranes and interfere with cellular processes. Secondary nucleation is a major source of these toxic species.

Simulation Controls

  • Concentration: Initial monomer count
  • Primary Nucleation: Rate of de novo seed formation
  • Secondary Nucleation: Rate of surface-catalyzed nucleation
  • Elongation: Monomer addition rate to fibril ends
  • Fragmentation: Fibril breakage rate

Key Concepts

  • Enzyme Kinetics: Enzymes catalyze reactions following Michaelis-Menten kinetics, with rates determined by substrate concentration and enzyme affinity (Km).
  • Lock and Key vs Induced Fit: Enzymes recognize substrates either through rigid complementarity (lock-key) or conformational changes upon binding (induced fit).
  • Allosteric Regulation: Enzyme activity modulated by molecules binding at sites other than the active site, enabling sophisticated metabolic control.
  • Signal Transduction: Cascades of molecular interactions that amplify and transmit signals from cell surface to nucleus.
