MAPK Phosphorylation Cascades

The MAPK (Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinase) cascade is a prototypical three-tier phosphorylation cascade that transmits growth factor signals from the cell surface to the nucleus.

The Three-Tier Cascade

  • MAPKKK (RAF): Activated by Ras-GTP at plasma membrane
  • MAPKK (MEK1/2): Phosphorylated by RAF on two serine residues
  • MAPK (ERK1/2): Dual phosphorylation on Thr-Glu-Tyr (TEY) motif

Scaffold Proteins (KSR)

Scaffold proteins like KSR (Kinase Suppressor of Ras) organize the cascade:

  • Bind RAF, MEK, and ERK simultaneously
  • Increase local concentration of kinases
  • Enhance signal specificity and speed
  • Biphasic effect: too much scaffold inhibits signaling (kinase sequestration)

Dual Phosphorylation

ERK requires phosphorylation on both Thr and Tyr to become active. This provides:

  • Kinetic proofreading (reduces spurious activation)
  • Switch-like response (ultrasensitivity)
  • Enables long-distance signal propagation

Feedback Regulation

  • Negative: Active ERK phosphorylates RAF and SOS, reducing signal
  • Positive: ERK can enhance its own activation in some contexts
  • Phosphatases: PP2A, DUSP proteins dephosphorylate cascade members

Clinical Relevance

RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK pathway mutations drive ~40% of cancers. MEK inhibitors (trametinib) and BRAF inhibitors (vemurafenib) are used in melanoma treatment.

About This Simulation

Key Concepts

  • Enzyme Kinetics: Enzymes catalyze reactions following Michaelis-Menten kinetics, with rates determined by substrate concentration and enzyme affinity (Km).
  • Lock and Key vs Induced Fit: Enzymes recognize substrates either through rigid complementarity (lock-key) or conformational changes upon binding (induced fit).
  • Allosteric Regulation: Enzyme activity modulated by molecules binding at sites other than the active site, enabling sophisticated metabolic control.
  • Signal Transduction: Cascades of molecular interactions that amplify and transmit signals from cell surface to nucleus.

Why It Matters

Molecular understanding enables drug design, metabolic engineering, and synthetic biology.
