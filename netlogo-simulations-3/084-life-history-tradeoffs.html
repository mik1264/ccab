Development transforms a single cell into a complex organism through coordinated cell division, differentiation, and morphogenesis. This simulation models key developmental mechanisms.

About This Simulation

Implement fish or reptile scale development with size gradients and local interactions.

Key Concepts

  • Morphogen Gradients: Chemical signals that diffuse through tissue, creating concentration gradients that specify cell fates based on position.
  • Pattern Formation: Spontaneous emergence of spatial organization through reaction-diffusion, cell sorting, and mechanical forces.
  • Cell Signaling: Communication between cells through direct contact or secreted molecules, coordinating development across tissues.
  • Gene Regulatory Networks: Interconnected genes that control developmental timing and cell fate decisions.

Why It Matters

Understanding development informs regenerative medicine, birth defect prevention, and tissue engineering.

How to Explore

  • Adjust the sliders to modify simulation parameters and observe how the system responds
  • Look for emergent patterns that arise from agent interactions
  • Try extreme parameter values to find phase transitions and tipping points
