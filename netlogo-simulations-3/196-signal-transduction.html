G-Protein Coupled Receptor Signal Transduction

GPCRs (G-Protein Coupled Receptors) are the largest family of membrane receptors, mediating responses to hormones, neurotransmitters, light, and odors. About 34% of FDA-approved drugs target GPCRs.

Signaling Cascade

  1. Ligand Binding: Agonist binds receptor, inducing conformational change
  2. G-Protein Activation: GDP→GTP exchange on Gα; Gα separates from Gβγ
  3. Effector Activation: Gαs activates adenylyl cyclase (AC)
  4. Second Messenger: AC converts ATP to cAMP
  5. Kinase Activation: cAMP activates protein kinase A (PKA)
  6. Phosphorylation: PKA phosphorylates target proteins

Signal Amplification

Each stage amplifies the signal dramatically:

  • 1 active receptor → hundreds of G-proteins
  • 1 G-protein → thousands of cAMP molecules
  • 1 PKA → many phosphorylated targets

Signal Termination

  • GTPase: Gα hydrolyzes GTP → GDP, self-inactivation
  • Phosphodiesterase: Degrades cAMP to AMP
  • Phosphatases: Remove phosphate groups from targets
  • Desensitization: Receptor phosphorylation, internalization

G-Protein Types

  • Gαs: Stimulates adenylyl cyclase (shown here)
  • Gαi: Inhibits adenylyl cyclase
  • Gαq: Activates phospholipase C → Ca²⁺ signaling
