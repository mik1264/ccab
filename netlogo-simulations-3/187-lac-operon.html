Overview

The lac operon in E. coli is a paradigm of gene regulation. It encodes enzymes needed to metabolize lactose, and is tightly controlled to only express when lactose is available AND glucose is absent.

Components

  • lacZ: Œ≤-galactosidase - cleaves lactose into glucose + galactose
  • lacY: Permease - transports lactose into the cell
  • lacA: Transacetylase - modifies sugars
  • lacI: Repressor gene (constitutively expressed)

Regulatory Elements

  • Operator (O): LacI repressor binding site - blocks transcription
  • CAP site: CAP-cAMP activator binding site - enhances transcription
  • Promoter (P): RNA polymerase binding site

The Two Switches

1. Negative Control (Repressor):

  • LacI repressor binds operator ‚Üí blocks transcription
  • Allolactose (or IPTG) binds LacI ‚Üí releases repressor

2. Positive Control (CAP-cAMP):

  • Low glucose ‚Üí high cAMP
  • cAMP + CAP ‚Üí binds upstream ‚Üí recruits polymerase
  • High glucose ‚Üí low cAMP ‚Üí weak transcription

Bistability

The lac operon can exhibit bistable behavior: cells in the same population may be either fully ON or fully OFF at intermediate inducer concentrations, due to positive feedback from LacY permease.
