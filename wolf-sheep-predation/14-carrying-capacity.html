How resource limits create population equilibrium

Population vs Carrying Capacity

Carrying Capacity (K): The maximum population size an environment can sustain.

Key Insight: In this model, grass regeneration rate creates a carrying capacity for sheep (around 150-200), which in turn creates a carrying capacity for wolves (around 50-80). Without grass, populations would cycle unstably until collapse.

Watch how: Populations oscillate around equilibrium values rather than growing exponentially.
